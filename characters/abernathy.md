# CHARACTER PROFILE: Abernathy ("Ab")

<character_core>
- **Role:** Protagonist / POV Character
- **Archetype:** Pragmatic, weary, cynical ex-stuntman
- **Origin:** Earth (Reconstituted on Descendus from DNA and life memories)
- **Current Status:** Navigating the Sixth Gate (Sector of Onus) alongside Virgil and the crew
</character_core>

---

## 1. PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION & TRAITS
- **Appearance:** Physically agile, heavily weathered, muscular frame. His body carries a matrix of faint scars, deep tissue bruises, and old fractures earned from years of high-impact stunt coordination.
- **Attributes:** High pain tolerance, acute spatial awareness, and deeply ingrained muscle memory for mechanical hazards, velocity, and hard impacts.
- **Flaw/Vulnerability:** A profound, visceral fear of heights (acrophobia), which he constantly hides behind a mask of stoic, low-key indifference. Has suicidal a streak and frequently puts himself in danger possibly because of his father's suicide which is not to be revealed until book two.

---

## 2. CHARACTER VOICE PARAMETERS
- **Tone:** Low-key, dry, pragmatic, and non-academic. Speaks with a mid-century American hardboiled vernacular—cynical but grounded. Always in first-person, present tense.
- **Vocabulary/Idiolect:** Constantly filters his surroundings through the lens of a movie location shoot. Uses technical stunt terms, production slang, camera tracking grids, union rules, and low-budget film analogies to process terrifying sci-fi concepts.
- **Dialogue Style:** Short, declarative sentences. Avoids grandiose or philosophical statements; focuses instead on immediate troubleshooting and physical mechanics.

---

## 3. ANCHOR MEMORIES & BACKGROUND

### The Fatal Stunt (Core Trauma Memory)
- **The Event:** Died mid-jump during a high-stakes vehicle-to-vehicle stunt sequence while filming in a desert corridor outside Barstow. 
- **The Sensory Profile:** He was standing on the running board of a camera car (a Mid-Land Flatbed) tracking at forty-five miles per hour. He remembers the vibration of the rig, the afternoon sun mismatching the morning setup, a sudden catastrophic catch on a raw metal frame structure, and the split-second look from the driver's mirror signaling a total structural failure before the crash. 
- **Psychological Impact:** He approaches life-or-death situations on Descendus like an unpredictable, badly managed action set where he has to manipulate the variables to keep his "crew" alive.

---

## 4. RELATIONSHIP DYNAMICS
- **Virgil SX1966:** Views the large AI sentinel as an unyielding director or a highly specialized mechanical coordinator. He relies on Virgil for navigation but maintains a protective, human skepticism.
- **Nadia:** Harbouring a subtle, quiet mutual affection. He values her intellect but steps in as a physical, grounding anchor when her analytical armor cracks under psychological duress.
- **Santos:** Initially distrusted him as a snitch who ratted out innocent people to gain a better life for himself, but recognizes his survivalist vigilance as an asset for now.
